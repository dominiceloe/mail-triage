Privacy Policy

mail-triage

Overview

mail-triage is a client-side web application that runs entirely in your browser. It does not collect, store, or transmit any of your data to any server.

What the app accesses

When you sign in with Google, the app requests permission to read and modify your Gmail messages. This access is used solely to:

• Read your inbox to count and categorize unread emails
• Archive emails by removing the INBOX label (equivalent to pressing "Archive" in Gmail)
• Optionally mark archived emails as read

All of this happens directly between your browser and Gmail's API. No intermediary server is involved.

Data collection

mail-triage collects no data. There is no backend, no database, no server, and no logging. Nothing about your emails, your account, or your usage is stored or transmitted anywhere.

Analytics and tracking

There are no analytics, no cookies, no tracking pixels, and no third-party scripts beyond the Google API client required for Gmail access.

Third parties

No third party receives any of your data. The only external services the app communicates with are Google's authentication and Gmail API endpoints, directly from your browser.

Data retention

mail-triage retains no data. When you close the browser tab, nothing persists. Your Gmail access token is held in memory only for the duration of your session.
